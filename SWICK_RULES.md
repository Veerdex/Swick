# The Rules of SWICK

*A trick-taking card game for 3–6 players. Created by Gary L. Koupal, 4/12/99.*

---

## The Deck

- Standard deck with all 2s through 6s and Jokers removed
- **32 cards total:** 7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A in all four suits (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣)
- **Card rank (high to low):** A K Q J 10 9 8 7
- The 7 is the lowest card in normal play

---

## The Pot

- The pot must always be **divisible by 3** (because there are 3 tricks)
- Each trick is worth exactly **1/3 of the pot**
- **Minimum ante:** 3¢ per player
- **Dealer always antes 3¢ extra** on top of the standard ante

**Example (3 players, 3¢ ante):**
| Player | Pays |
|--------|------|
| Player 1 | 3¢ |
| Player 2 | 3¢ |
| Dealer (Player 3) | 6¢ (3¢ + 3¢ extra) |
| **Total pot** | **12¢** |

The dealer sets the ante amount before cards are dealt. Players cannot join if they won't ante the required amount. The dealer is responsible for ensuring the pot is correct — if it's short, the dealer covers the difference.

---

## Objective

Win at least one trick (1/3 of the pot). Players who knock in but fail to win a trick **go set** and must match the pot for the next hand.

---

## Special Winning Hands

These beat all other hands and win the entire pot immediately:

| Rank | Hand |
|------|------|
| 1st (best) | Three Aces (any suits) |
| 2nd | Three 7s (any suits) |
| 3rd | Ace-King-Queen of Trump |

> Three Aces and Three 7s also beat A-K-Q of Trump.

---

## Starting the Game

1. Any player shuffles the deck 3 times and deals one card face-up to each player in turn
2. The **first player to receive an Ace** becomes the first dealer
3. The dealer rotates left each hand

---

## Sequence of Play Each Hand

### 1. Ante Up

All players who want to play ante the amount set by the dealer. The dealer antes the standard amount **plus 3¢ extra**.

### 2. The Deal

- Dealer shuffles at least 3 times; player to the right cuts the deck
- Deal **3 cards face-down** to each player, one at a time, going left to right
- After all players have 3 cards, the dealer flips the top remaining card — this is the **trump card**

### 3. Trump Selection (Dealer Only)

The dealer sees the trump card and decides:

- **Keep it:** Place the trump card face-up on top of your 3 face-down cards. The trump suit is now public. This card becomes part of your hand — **it cannot be discarded.**
- **Pass on it:** Place the trump card face-up on the table. Trump suit is still public, but the dealer doesn't get the extra card.

> **Critical rule:** The dealer does NOT look at their 3 dealt cards until after all other players have knocked in and discarded/drawn. The dealer is blind until their discard phase.

### 4. Knock-In Phase

Starting with the player to the dealer's left and going clockwise, each player decides:

- **Knock** (knuckles on the table) = "I'm in" — you commit to playing the hand
- **"I pass"** = you're out — your ante is lost but you have no further obligations this hand.

The dealer decides last (after all non-dealers).

> If the dealer already kept the trump card and then chooses not to play, the dealer goes **set single** (must match the pot).

> If everyone except the dealer passes, the dealer wins the pot automatically.

### 5. Discard & Draw Phase

Starting with the player to the dealer's left (clockwise), each knocked-in player may:

- Keep all 3 cards ("I'll play with these")
- Discard 1, 2, or all 3 cards and draw that many new cards from the deck

After all non-dealers have gone, the **dealer** may now pick up their cards (including the trump card if kept) and look at them for the first time. The dealer may then discard and draw, with one rule: **the trump card cannot be discarded.**

> If the dealer kept the trump card and discards all 3 other cards + draws 3 new ones, they now hold 4 cards. They must then discard 1 more card (not the trump) to get back to 3.

---

## Special Dealer Rule: Face Trump Card

If the dealer kept the trump card **and** it is a face card (J, Q, K, or A), the dealer must announce before the trick-taking phase whether they will:

- **"Play these cards"** — commit to playing and must win **2 tricks** or go Set Double
- **"Set Single"** — voluntarily drop out, match the pot for next hand, and all trick winners or non-set players get a free ride next hand

If the dealer kept a **low trump card** (7, 8, 9, 10), they only need to win **1 trick** to avoid going set.

---

## Trick-Taking

### Lead Card Rule (First Trick Only)

If the player immediately left of the dealer was dealt the **Ace of Trump**, they **must** lead with it on the very first play of the entire game. This rule only applies to the first trick of each hand.

### Playing a Trick

1. The lead player plays any card face-up
2. Going clockwise, each player **must follow suit** if able, and must play a **higher card of the same suit** if they have one
3. If you **cannot follow suit**, you must play a **trump card** if you have one
4. If you have **neither** the lead suit nor a trump, play any card

### Winning a Trick

- A trump card always beats a non-trump card
- If multiple trumps are played, the highest trump wins
- If no trump is played, the highest card of the **lead suit** wins
- The trick winner leads the next trick and collects 1/3 of the pot

### Three Tricks

Three tricks are played per hand. Each is worth 1/3 of the pot.

---

## Going Set

A player who **knocked in** but **failed to win a trick** has "gone set."

| Situation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Standard player goes set | Match the pot (single) |
| Dealer kept low trump (7–10) and wins 0 tricks | Match the pot (single) |
| Dealer kept face trump (J/Q/K/A) and wins 0 or 1 trick | **Double the pot** (set double) |
| Dealer voluntarily goes "Set Single" before playing | Match the pot (single) |

> **Key rule:** If anyone goes set, the other players who won tricks do **not** pay any ante next hand — they get a free ride. Only the new dealer antes their standard 3¢ extra.

The set amount carries over to the **next hand's pot**. The pot can grow substantially over many hands.

---

## Ending the Game

- Players agree in advance on an end time or condition
- **A game cannot end while a set is in play.** Play must continue until no player goes set and the pot is cleanly divided among trick winners.

---

## Quick Reference

```
DECK:        32 cards — 7 through A in all 4 suits
PLAYERS:     3–6
DEAL:        3 cards each + 1 trump card flipped
TRICKS:      3 per hand (each worth 1/3 pot)
WIN:         Win ≥1 trick (or 2 if dealer kept face trump)
SET:         Miss required tricks → match (or double) the pot next hand
SPECIAL:     3 Aces > 3 Sevens > A-K-Q of Trump → instant pot win
```
